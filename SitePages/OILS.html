Organisational Intentional Learning Structures (OIL'S)

.

Intentional Learning

Last updated/Quality Checked, Jan, 2022

Intentional learning is the practice of treating every experience as an opportunity to learn something. So instead of learning being something off to the side that happens at pre-prescribed times, it is encouraged to be an output of every event, interaction and deliverable. In effect every daily experience carries with it the opportunity to learn and grow

Mindset

Last updated/Quality Checked, Jan, 2022

It is generally accepted that the mindset most associated with intentional learners is one of curiosity and growth. A mindset that truly understands that no question is irrelevant.

It is our view that this mindset can be encouraged and enhanced by putting in place the right organisational structure in tandem with those that develop naturally.

Benefits

Last updated/Quality Checked, Jan, 2022

There are benefits for both the individual and the organisation when the organisation actively supports intentional learning over incidental.

  • The individual gets to keep abreast of trends and make connections between personal and professional activities and tools which not only helps them learn and grow but also makes them more attractive, employable and sought after. If we are indeed in for another industrial revolution, these people will lead it
  • The organisation gets a network of people with diverse skills that can not only enhance what they currently offer but also create that next offering that will differentiate them in the market place

Achieving Organisational Intentional Learning

Last updated/Quality Checked, Jan, 2022

  • Encourage curiosity
  • Encourage a growth mindset
  • Create the structures - that support diverse types of learning with a strong focus on community based learning
  • Implement a way of working - that includes learning from events in the fast feedback loops

Measuring

Last updated/Quality Checked, Jan, 2022

Like everything mindset related, there are qualitative measures. However in this case there is an extensive set of quantitive measures too

  1. Qualitative measures may include
    1. Do people see everyday events as learning opportunities (select a few obvious and less obvious events)
    2. Does the team/program/organisation encourage questions from everyone on everything
    3. Do employees view learning positively
  2. Quantitative measures may include
    1. Does the organisation encourage retrospectives and at which levels (team, project/product, solution/program…)
    2. Does the organisation encourage community learning events and which types (Communities of Practice, Hacks Days, Circles, Lunch & Learn…)
    3. Does the organisation have defined internal self service courses and instructor led courses
    4. Does the organisation have a budget per employee for formal training
    5. Does the organisation have defined internal self service courses and instructor led courses
    6. Do employees seek learning on a regular basis (daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, yearly)
