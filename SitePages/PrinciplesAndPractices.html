Overview

Last updated/Quality Checked, Feb, 2022

Principles are the 'Why' in our way or working, Practices are the 'How'. The 'Why' must always be looked at and understood else the 'How' could be causing more damage than we know. When the 'Why' is understood and agreed to, the 'How', although often difficult to execute, becomes easy to understand and articulate.
A principle is a fundamental truth or proposition that serves as the foundation for a system of belief or behaviour or for a chain of reasoning
A Practice is the customary, habitual, or expected procedure or way of doing of something
An example:

  • Why do we want to maxamise Flow?
    • Because maxamising flow helps maxamise throughput which helps achieve objectives and outcomes
  • How do we maxamise Flow?
    • Break things down i.e. the backlog model
    • Commit to small batch i.e. Sprint and/or WIP
    • Subordinate everything to the bottleneck i.e. dont overproduce upstream of a bottleneck as it equates to waste e.g. Huge overally elaborated backlog
In this page, we will go through all the Lean, Agile and DevOps Pronciples that we thouroughly support in a way that makes understanding the practical application of these principles easier.

Principle - Accountability

The art of "its my job"
Last updated/Quality Checked, Feb, 2022

Accountability comes in many forms. It can be for what you directly committed to. It can be for what your Team committed to. It can even be for what your Product/Project group committed to. How many times have you heard "Thats x's or y's job" even when the task is a bottleneck. In a Lean-Agile or DevXOps environment thats only true if X or Y have capacity and you have higher priority work to do. Otherwise, it is your job and if you dont know how to do it, now is as good a time as any to learn, within reason.
